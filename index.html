Cristiano Ronaldo

A Force to Reckon with

Cristiano Ronaldo Representing Portugal in the just concluded Euro.

A Brief time line that shows the life of CR7 thus far:

  • 1985 - Born in Madeira Portugal.
  • 1992 - Ronaldo started his youth career with Andorinha, through to 1995.
  • 1995 - He moved to Nacional and spent two football seasons with the Portuguese club side.
  • 1997 - In this year, he joined Sporting CP, a major club side that dominated and has great reputation both in Portugal and in European football competition. His relentlessness and hard work on and off the pitch attracted other big teams from Europe.
  • 2003 - In 2003, he joined Manchester United where he spent six(6) years featuring in 196 appearances and scoring 84 goals. During this period, he won his first Ballon d'Or.
  • 2009 - Real Madrid, if not the biggest club side in the world, required his services for a record transfer fee of 80 million Pounds, his unveiling ceremony was witnessed by a whooping 80,000 spectators at the Bernabeu. Surpassing a record held by Diego Maradonna when he joined Napoli.
  • 2010 - His career continued to soar, when in the 2010-11 season for the first time in his career he scored 4goals in a single game.
  • 2011 - He scored 60goals in all competition, was nominated for the ballon D'or once again, but was runner up to Lionel Messi. This never made him give up in his pursuit for success.
  • 2013 - Still conquering and achiving greater feat, he ended the year with 69 goals in 59 appearances his highest year-end goal tally. He received the 2013 FIFA Ballon d'Or, an amalgamation of the Ballon d'Or and the FIFA World Player of the Year award, for the first time in his career.
  • 2015 - At the start of his seventh season at Real Madrid, the 2015–16 campaign, Ronaldo became the club's all-time top scorer, first in the league and then in all competitions. His five-goal haul in a 6–0 away win over Espanyol on 12 September took his tally in La Liga to 230 goals in 203 games, surpassing the club's previous recordholder, Raúl.[187] A month later, on 17 October, he again surpassed Raúl when he scored the second goal in a 3–0 defeat of Levante at the Bernabéu to take his overall total for the club to 324 goals.
  • 2016 - He and his portuguese team mates defeated host nation France in the Euro 2016 tournament to be crowned champions of Europe.
  • 2018 - on 10 July 2018, Ronaldo signed a four-year contract with Italian club Juventus after completing a €100 million transfer, which included an additional €12 million in other fees and solidarity contributions to Ronaldo's youth clubs.
  • 2019 - Ronaldo scored his first Serie A hat-trick on 6 January 2020, in a 4–0 home win against Cagliari. His 56th career hat-trick, he became only the second player after Alexis Sánchez to score hat-tricks in the Premier League, La Liga, and Serie A.
  • 2021 - Portugal's Cristiano Ronaldo has done it again. Despite seeing his team knocked out in the Round of 16, he managed to win the award for top scorer at Euro 2021 with five goals and one assist Also he equaled the record for most career men's international goals during Euro 2020. He has now scored 109 goals on the international stage, matching a record set by Iran's Ali Daei.
